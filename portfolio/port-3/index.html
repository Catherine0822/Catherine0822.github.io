User Experience Design ~ NTUbike

By Catherine 23 December 2020

1. In-depth Interviw:

Interview with subjects to find out potential user’s needs. We found that most NTU students have uncomfortable biking experiences.

2. Field study:

Observed bicycle users in the campus of NTU. Record the journey.

3. Group similar behaviors:

Wrote down each behaviors observed in the field study on stickers, and classified them in groups. behaviors that were similar would be placed in the same group.

4. Persona Behavioral Variables:

Placed opposite descriptions of each variables on the two side of the scale, and placed every person we oberved during the field study onto every scale. By identifying behavioural variables, we grouped similar people together into types of users.

5. Persona Candidates:

By using the result from step 4, we easily create four persona candidates.

6. Decide Main Persona:

Chose one main persona from our persona candidates. The main persona would represent the target users who we wanted the solved problems for.

7. Stakeholders Map:

Wrote down the stakeholders of our main persona. Stakeholders that were more closed to the middle could impact the persoma more.

8. Experience Journey Map:

Wrote down the doing, thinking, feeling of our persona in a bicycle journey, from getting bike, riding bike, to parking bike. The experience journey map allows us to easily found out user’s problems.
